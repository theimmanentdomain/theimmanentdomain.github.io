"The Duck"- A soprano fancfiction by E.I.Pankov

The coke hits first. That’s how it always starts. Fast. Sharp. Tony feels his jaw lock up, his chest
tighten, his fingers moving faster than his thoughts. He’s talking loud—too loud—about Paulie
still owing him money from three goddamn years ago. Silvio nods, leans in, but his face is
shifting, morphing between light and shadow. The neon hums in the Bada Bing, you can smell
the garbage collected outside it hasn't been taken out yet. He takes another drink. It tastes like
gasoline.

Then the ketamine creeps in, slow like molasses. The floor is gone. Just gone. He’s still sitting,
but he’s also sinking, weightless and heavy all at once. The girls on stage blur together, their
arms trailing behind them in waves of light. Everything is moving, except his body. His mind
detaches, hovering just a few feet above his own head, watching himself in real time.
Christopher is saying something, eyes darting, that quick nervous energy, but Tony doesn’t hear
it. His ears are full of static. The walls stretch and breathe.

And then—then come the mushrooms. The final guest at the party, the one who turns the music
down and whispers secrets that shouldn’t be heard. Everything is too big and too small. Silvio’s
sneer loops infinitely, like a gif from a nightmare. Tony’s hands, Jesus, they won’t stop changing
sizes. One moment they’re fists like anvils, the next they’re pale, delicate, soft like his mother’s.
And that’s when he sees it.

A duck. Standing dead center in the club. Looking right at him.

Tony opens his mouth to speak, but no words come out. His tongue is too big. His teeth feel like
they don’t belong to him. The duck tilts its head.

“Tony,” it says, in a voice that shakes the foundation of the world, “you can’t keep running from
this.”

And he knows. He knows what the duck means. He tries to stand, to do something, anything,
but the ketamine has swallowed his legs whole. Paulie is beside him, or maybe it’s Paulie’s
ghost, whispering in his ear:

“All of this? It’s just a dream, T. A f*ckin’ bad episode on repeat.”

Tony looks down at his hands. They’re covered in blood. Thick, wet, real. The lights in the Bing
flicker, the music distorts, slows down, like a record melting on a turntable. The air smells like
burning hair. He blinks and his mother’s face is in front of him, hovering like a goddamn specter.
He tries to scream, but his voice is gone.

And then—the realization.

He is the duck.


All this time, he thought the ducks leaving his pool were about his family. No. The ducks were
him. He is the thing that flies away. The thing that cannot be held onto. The thing that will never
belong to anything, to anyone.

And it terrifies him.

An hour later, the comedown hits like a baseball bat to the skull. He’s staring at the ceiling of the
Bada Bing. Christopher is still talking, but his voice is muffled, like it’s coming from inside a fish
tank. The ceiling tiles shift in and out of focus, and for a moment, he swears they are watching
him.

The next morning, he doesn’t talk about it. Not to Christopher. Not to Paulie. Not even to Dr.
Melfi. He watches AJ move through the house and feels a strange detachment, like he’s
watching a play he’s already seen before. He steps outside, lights a cigar. A bird lands on the
fence. His heart skips a beat. For a moment, he swears it’s looking at him.

Then he shrugs it off. Goes about his day. But deep down, he knows.

He will never be the same.
