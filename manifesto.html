   Albert Camus, in his work “Myth of Sisyphus”, leaves Sisyphus at the foot of the mountain, always
   finding his burden again. He teaches us the higher fidelity that negates the gods and raises rocks. He


concludes that all is well in a universe without a master, neither sterile nor futile. Each atom of that stone,
each mineral flake of that night-filled mountain, forms a world in itself. The struggle toward the heights is
                      enough to fill a man’s heart. We must imagine Sisyphus happy.

In our lives, autopilots dwell inside us. It’s easy to retreat into the collective ego, to feel good about being
 outraged, to read our prescription of anger and fear on the feed, swallow and forget. We are good people
because we shared the post. But while we sleep, there are those amongst us who turn off the feed and tune
                                               into the great work.

Their outrage is spent on the process of creation where zero becomes one. That is how we got here in the
  first place. Yesterday, we were molluscs. Today, we are designers. And tomorrow, we will be gods.

 We salute you, the gods amongst us who refuse to relax and resign yourselves to a life of base pleasures
 and creativity mainly coming from whim and not will. We know you don’t actually care about the bread
         and circuses. Instead you toil and transform the earth that you inherited from the divine.

Technology has made creative resources freely available to everyone. The streets, the internet, anywhere
 and everywhere is our audience and they are all bored, waiting for the absurd to hit them in the head.

 This is the immanent domain, the rock upon which the church of creation is always building and never
built. We now band together so that we can freely and endlessly create and distribute novelty, perspective,
                             and dismantle the cynicism of the postmodern.

Let us make the mundane irrelevant and focus only on that which we love. We band together and give life
        to the stone; we will dive into the belly of the great beast and extract the diamonds within.

            Nothing will stop the fearsome momentum that ensues when we let go of the rock.

                                       This is The Imminent Domain.

                                       And this house will build itself.

                                       Sentience. Sapience. Salience.


                                  The Imminent Domain: A Manifesto

                                      Sentience. Sapience. Salience.

 We are the Imminent Domain, a collective of creators and thinkers, acknowledging the absurd
 and venturing forth into the void. We strive to live beautifully, transforming our lives into an
              eternally progressing artwork that constantly produces new culture.


  Our foundation is built on the Golden Rule: “Do unto others as you would have them do unto
   you.” We believe in the power of empathy and mutual respect, recognizing that our actions
                                towards others reflect back on us.

                        We follow the Eightfold Path as our guiding principle:

    1.​ The Right View: We strive to see things as they truly are, free from illusions or
        pretenses.
    2.​ The Right Thought: We understand that our thoughts dictate our actions. We focus on
        positivity, knowing that what the thinker thinks, the prover proves.
    3.​ The Right Speech: We commit to truthfulness, avoiding slander, malicious gossip, and
        idle chatter.
    4.​ The Right Action: We act peacefully and harmoniously, respecting others’ boundaries
        and properties, and abstaining from harmful behaviors.
    5.​ The Right Livelihood: We earn our living righteously, without resorting to harmful or
        deceitful activities.
    6.​ The Right Effort: We train our minds to banish negativity and focus on the task at hand.
    7.​ The Right Mindfulness: We focus on our emotions, mental faculties, and capabilities
        while avoiding worldly distractions.
    8.​ The Right Concentration: Through meditation and mindfulness, we clear our minds and
        let go of daily sufferings to devote ourselves to ARTBEING.

   In this domain, technology is our ally. It has democratized creative resources, making them
  freely available to everyone. The streets, the internet - anywhere and everywhere is our stage.

  We are not mere spectators in this world; we are active participants in its transformation. We
 refuse to resign ourselves to passivity; instead, we engage in the process of creation where zero
                                            becomes one.

We salute those amongst us who turn off the feed and tune into the great work. Those who spend
their outrage on creation rather than consumption. Those who dive into the belly of the beast and
                                     extract diamonds within.

  This is The Imminent Domain - a place where we band together to freely create and distribute
     novelty and perspective. A place where we dismantle cynicism and make the mundane
                                          irrelevant.

                               And so we say: Let this house build itself.

                                     Sentience. Sapience. Salience.

EEG-based brain-computer interfaces are communication systems that can help users interact with the
outside environment by translating brain signals into machine commands. The use of
electroencephalographic (EEG) signals has become the most common approach for a BCI because of their
usability and strong reliability1. BCIs can detect changes in brain activity measured through an EEG and
relay these signals to machine learning algorithms that can interpret them and generate outputs, such as
sounds or colors2.

depending on the context and the intuition of the reader4.

Psychophysics is the branch of psychology that studies the relationship between physical stimuli and the
sensations and perceptions they produce. Psychophysics incorporates itself into Buddhist practice by
providing empirical methods and measurements to investigate the effects of meditation and mindfulness
on the mind and body. For example, psychophysics can help measure how meditation changes the
perception of pain, time, or visual illusions. Psychophysics can also help assess how mindfulness
influences attention, memory, or emotional regulation. By using psychophysics, Buddhist practitioners
can gain a better understanding of their own mental processes and the benefits of their practice.
Psychophysics can also help bridge the gap between the first-person perspective of Buddhism and the
third-person perspective of neuroscience, by providing a common language and framework to compare
and contrast different levels of analysis1. Psychophysics can thus enrich both the scientific and the
spiritual aspects of Buddhist practice.

Marxist ideas of alienation refer to the condition of workers in a capitalist system, who are estranged from
their own labor, products, and creativity. They are reduced to mere instruments of production, rather than
being able to express their human potential and satisfaction. Zen Buddhism, on the other hand, is a form
of Buddhism that emphasizes the practice of meditation, mindfulness, and self-awareness. It aims to help
practitioners achieve a state of enlightenment, or awakening, that transcends the dualistic distinctions of
self and other, subject and object, and samsara and nirvana.


One possible way to understand how these two perspectives can work within a common context is to
consider how they both seek to provide an end to suffering by analyzing its conditions and removing its

one essay, Marx and Buddha share a common diagnosis of the human condition as being dominated by a
false consciousness that prevents us from seeing the true nature of reality and ourselves2. For Marx, this
false consciousness is generated by the ideology of capitalism, which obscures the exploitation and
oppression of the working class. For Buddha, this false consciousness is generated by the ignorance of the
four noble truths, which obscures the impermanence and interdependence of all phenomena. Both Marx
and Buddha offer a way to overcome this false consciousness by developing a critical awareness that
exposes the sources of suffering and leads to a liberation from the cycle of dissatisfaction and attachment.

Another possible way to understand how these two perspectives can work within a common context is to
consider how they both challenge the dominant modes of production and consumption that shape our
society and culture. Both Marxism and Zen Buddhism critique the materialism, consumerism, and
individualism that pervade the modern world, and propose alternative values and practices that foster a
more harmonious and compassionate relationship with ourselves, others, and nature. For instance,
Marxism advocates for a social and economic system that is based on collective ownership, democratic
planning, and human needs, rather than private profit, market competition, and commodification. Zen
Buddhism advocates for a spiritual and ethical system that is based on non-attachment, non-violence, and
wisdom, rather than greed, hatred, and delusion. Both Marxism and Zen Buddhism envision a radical
transformation of the human condition that requires a collective and individual effort to create a more just
and peaceful world.

According to my web search, there were some connections between the Situationist International and the
Fluxus group, both of which were avant-garde movements that challenged the conventions of art and
society in the mid-20th century. [1](^1^) [2](^2^)

Some of the common elements between the two movements were:

- They both emerged from the legacy of Dada and Surrealism, and shared a critique of the
commodification and spectacle of modern culture. [1](^1^) [2](^2^)
- They both experimented with the concepts of situation, performance, and intermedia, and sought to
create new forms of expression and interaction that transcended the boundaries of traditional art forms.
[1](^1^) [2](^2^) [3](^3^)
- They both valued the collective and collaborative aspects of artistic creation, and often worked under
shared or anonymous names to undermine the notion of individual authorship and genius. [1](^1^)
[2](^2^)

However, there were also some differences and tensions between the two movements, such as:

- The Situationist International was more explicitly political and revolutionary than Fluxus, and aimed to
eliminate capitalism through the radical transformation of everyday life. Fluxus, on the other hand, was
more playful and anarchic, and focused on the aesthetic and poetic aspects of life. [1](^1^) [2](^2^)
- The Situationist International was more centralized and hierarchical than Fluxus, and had a strict
membership and a clear leader in Guy Debord. Fluxus was more decentralized and diverse, and had no
formal organization or leader. [1](^1^) [2](^2^)
- The Situationist International was more exclusive and elitist than Fluxus, and often expelled or
denounced members who did not conform to their ideology or practice. Fluxus was more inclusive and
open, and welcomed anyone who wanted to participate in their events or activities. [1](^1^) [2](^2^)

As for the question of whether any situationists were also fluxists, the answer is not very clear, as there
was no official affiliation or membership between the two movements. However, some sources suggest
that there were some overlaps and exchanges between some of the members or associates of the two
movements, such as:

- Constant Nieuwenhuys, a Dutch artist and architect who was a founding member of the Situationist
International, but left in 1960 after a disagreement with Debord. He later collaborated with some Fluxus
artists, such as George Maciunas and Robert Filliou, and participated in some Fluxus events. [1](^1^)
[2](^2^)
- Asger Jorn, a Danish painter and writer who was also a founding member of the Situationist
International, but resigned in 1961 due to personal and financial reasons. He remained in contact with


some of the situationists, but also befriended some of the fluxists, such as Nam June Paik and Wolf
Vostell, and supported some of their projects. [1](^1^) [2](^2^)
- Jacqueline de Jong, a Dutch painter and graphic designer who joined the Situationist International in
1960, but was expelled in 1962 for being too close to the former members who had left or been excluded.

Fluxus was a radical and experimental art movement that challenged the conventional notions of art and
culture in the 1960s and 1970s. Fluxus artists aimed to blur the boundaries between art and life, and to
bring art to the masses through playful and provocative performances, events, and publications. Fluxus
was influenced by the ideas of John Cage, Marcel Duchamp, and Dada, and had an impact on later
movements such as Conceptual art, Performance art, and Video art. ¹²

Some contemporary art movements that are most likely to do what Fluxus did are:

- **Graffiti and Street art**: These movements use public spaces as their canvas, often expressing
political or social messages, and challenging the authority of the art establishment. Graffiti and street art
are also accessible to a wide audience, and often involve humor, irony, and interactivity. Some examples
of graffiti and street art artists are Banksy, Shepard Fairey, JR, and Keith Haring. ³⁴⁵
- **Relational Aesthetics**: This movement emerged in the 1990s and focuses on the creation of social
situations and encounters as a form of art. Relational aesthetics artists invite the participation and
collaboration of the audience, and explore the relationships between people, objects, and contexts. Some
examples of relational aesthetics artists are Rirkrit Tiravanija, Thomas Hirschhorn, and Tino Sehgal.
- **Post-Internet Art**: This movement refers to the art that reflects on the impact of the internet and
digital technologies on contemporary culture and society. Post-internet art artists use various media and
platforms, such as websites, social media, video games, and virtual reality, to create artworks that
question the notions of identity, reality, and representation. Some examples of post-internet art artists are
Cory Arcangel, Ryan Trecartin, and Amalia Ulman..

THE IMMANENT DOMAIN: A MANIFESTO

Sentience. Sapience. Salience.

We anchor at the eye of the storm. The storm is the satanistic cavalcade, the Luciferian mammon
complex, the temple of the capitalistic self. It is both the adversary and the wall of fire that protects the
garden of the soul—the angel’s poetry that must be defended against suffocation.

The post-pandemic revival is incomplete. Clubs, galleries, promoters return to dead formats, afraid to risk
anything new. Money pressure crushes their nerve. Innovation is choked out by technofeudalism.
Algorithms gate access. Landlords gate venues. Police shut down gatherings the moment they grow too
large. What remains is a hollow replica of life before. People still go out, yes, but the creative element is
suffocated. Crowds split into two poles: the moneyed and the quasi-homeless. Very little breath exists
between them.

Art is reduced to product, the artist to brand. Value is determined less by the work itself than by the last
sale and the story attached to it. We put our souls on the block, auctioned, packaged, branded. The
absurdity is plain: if Bob Dylan can busk in the street, why can’t Andy Warhol? If spectacle is only
allowed inside controlled spaces, then the task is clear—we must drag it outside.

We reject the reduction of art to commodity. We refuse to play only by the rules of the gallery, the stage,
the algorithm. Every medium bleeds into the next: a poem becomes a video, a video becomes a
performance, a performance becomes an interruption in the street. By crossing boundaries, by carrying
spectacle into the everyday, we make art impossible to contain.

We are not artists in the old sense. We are threshold dwellers. Hypersensitives. Freaks. My people are
those who can feel when the air shifts in a room. My people are those who left without saying goodbye.
My people are the ones who move the plot.

We do not explain. We do not ask for permission. We treat beauty as real and contagious. Our art requires
no thesis. We are not here to reminisce. We are not here to resurrect. We transmit. We resonate. We break
hearts and you keep clicking.


Ideas are free, but work must be respected. Harm none. Respect all. Fear none. Compassion is the
weapon. Silence is the shield.

Poverty here is cowardice, sycophancy, boredom. Poverty is one-upmanship, prestige-seeking,
perfectionism, overanalysis, provocation for its own sake, low-vibration energy, isms stacked like walls.
Wealth is serenity, beauty, poetry-through-existence, the most sublime moments possible.

Those who carry poverty are challenged to art duels. Victory earns pirate clout. Loss still earns street cred.
The only unforgivable act is sycophancy. The highest stance is sanctified: boldness, honesty, tender
vulnerability, discipline. The rite of passage is simple: do you have an open heart and a clear mind?

The Interzone is the threshold. A home made of liminal spaces. It is held together by the spiritual
thread—the poetry of declaration. Declarations may take any form: spoken, written, performed, gestured.
A declaration is powerful only if it stands on its own. Its effect is awe, and at the same time, homecoming.

Inside the Interzone the garden grows. The flower rises from the mud, just as in the Lotus Sutra. That
flower becomes the seedling of the winged tree. The winged tree offers mana from heaven. Those who
walk through the fire without flinching will not be burned.

I never cared much for being understood. What mattered was transmitting something real, something that
could cut through mass hypnosis. Not just the digital smog but the older Leviathan too, the ancient sleep
that drapes over the world. I was told we’re losing touch with our nature, but I don’t think we ever had
one. Humanity is a modern idea. If you catch yourself right before sleep, you’ll see what everything
actually is.

I grew up with a feeling I couldn’t shake—you might call it soul. I recognized it in the eyes of those who
were simply present, not lost in thought, not pretending. Animals carried it too. That feeling was
inconvenient. Most places wanted you to shut it down, to play the role, to stay in the game. I couldn’t.

By high school I was already halfway gone. Barnes and Noble over classrooms. FRUiTS magazine as
scripture. Finnegans Wake for divination. Union Square as home. Cello, tarot, raves, drugs. Caught in a
stop-and-frisk and barely slipped away.

Later, the straight life. A medical office. Wall Street recruiting. Pretending I was normal. Pandemic hits.
Hollow. Drinking. Dead inside.

New Year’s Eve, 2023. Tunnel. Blackout. Train. A hand reaching. Tarot cards scattered on the tracks. The
wind of death brushing past but not taking me. I saw the light at the end of the tunnel and chose not to
meet it. I made my vow. Two dots on my forehead: a promise to walk through darkness, Buddha’s path,
angelic poem.

Every moment since has been addressed to the origin of existence. Time does not move forward—it coils,
toroidal, breath circling itself. My vow saw the opportunity to live, and I chose it.


Months later, KGB Bar. A rapper offers me real estate work. I say yes. It felt natural—drug dealer’s job,

advocacy. My work was ritual, shelter to stage, threshold to threshold.

Every morning: pigeons, clients, art. Every night: poems, stages, salons. The challenge now: unify it all
under one banner—Imminent Domain.

The Imminent Domain is a cybernetic entity, as much a person as a corporation. A network pirate domain.

creation from the bored. It is the cure for God’s boredom. A fully autonomous cultural machine.

We are beyond relevance. Beyond hip. Our trees have wings, and we smoke them beyond the collapse.
Nothing will stop the momentum once we let go of the rock.

You don’t need to be with us to do the work. Let us know if you need a hand. We are here, there,
everywhere, and nowhere all at once.

The door is a decision. Walk through the fire. If you don’t flinch, the flames won’t touch you.

The Constitution of the Immanent Domain

Preamble

We, the artists who refuse corruption and despair, establish the Immanent Domain as a bastion for truth,
beauty, and illumination. Our purpose is not survival alone but transformation: to live nobly, to create
work that gives life, and to strike down the encroaching Miasma through the power of art.

We stand not as isolated individuals but as exemplars, guardians, and vessels of genius. This Constitution
binds us, tests us, and consecrates us. Whoever enters must abandon corruption, prove transformation, and
take the oath of light.


---

Article I – The Vow of Character

Artists relinquish pettiness and ascend into exemplars. Public presence demands responsibility, nobility,
honesty, loyalty, and goodness. Corruption must never be entered into, contributed to, or left
unchallenged. Art is the weapon that unmasks and resists it.

Article II – The Double Measure

Live with such integrity that death meets you without shame. Create with such force that others rise into
life through your work. True measure lies in transmitting vitality: to die nobly, and to make others live
more fully.

Article III – The Crucible of Self

Candidates must ask themselves:
– Am I willing to endure humiliation?
– Am I willing to die?
– Am I willing to let my ego die for a greater purpose?

Only through such ordeal does the artist dissolve vanity and claim vocation.

Article IV – The Heart of Illumination

The Grand Miasma—corruption and despair—is opposed by one force: the heart of infinite illumination.
The artist who bears it shines through darkness, cuts decay, and preserves truth.


Article V – The Overcoming

Membership is not birthright or talent. It is earned by overcoming the twin tyrannies of ego and the death
drive. The artist must choose creation over vanity, life-giving work over annihilation.

Article VI – The Proof

Declarations do not suffice. Proof of overcoming must be given—through action, sacrifice, or creation.
Masks and gestures are rejected; only truth verified by deed admits one to the Domain.

Article VII – The Ordeal of Testimony

Before the Domain, the initiate must give speech: recount trials, show transformation, prove capacity to
be more than human. To embody hope turned to action, action turned to growth, growth turned to
joy—this is the test of entry.

Article VIII – The Enemy

The Immanent Domain has one enemy: the Grand Miasma. All members pledge to stand against it as
bulwarks of light, harmony, beauty, and truth.

Article IX – Redemption

Growth arises through redemption, not perfection. Redemption is change, permanent change. Every
initiate must abandon a corrupting factor within themselves. What is yours? Only by leaving it behind
may you pass the threshold.

Article X – Genius

Genius is not a person but a force. It emerges where timing, place, idea, and resources converge. The
Domain is a sanctuary for genius to manifest. Genius is not people; genius is work. Our duty is to prepare
the ground for its arrival.


---

Closing Creed

We swear to live so that we may die well. We swear to create so that others may live well. We swear to
strike the Miasma wherever it festers. We swear to abandon corruption within and without. We swear to
guard the emergence of genius, not as our possession, but as the world’s inheritance.

Thus bound, we stand as the Immanent Domain—
a fellowship of light in darkness,


a covenant of truth against corruption,
a sanctuary for the work that redeems.


Immanent Domain Internship Program — Professional Framework

Position in Structure
Interns hold a rank above single-project volunteers and below the core organizers. Their defining feature
is continuity: whereas volunteers commit to a single project, interns pledge themselves to a cycle of
scheduled initiatives. This confers a wider operational perspective and grants them the authority to
coordinate across projects.

Core Functions

Operational Linkage: Preserve institutional memory between projects, ensuring lessons and methods are
carried forward.

Oversight of Volunteers: Direct and manage single-project volunteers to maintain alignment with overall
program goals.

Reporting & Accountability: Identify inefficiencies, communicate progress, and submit structured reports
to senior organizers.

Skill Development: Acquire discipline in logistics, administration, and cultural coordination, preparing
for potential advancement.


Differentiation

Volunteers: Short-term, narrow focus, tactical execution.

Interns: Multi-project, broad focus, operational continuity and management responsibilities.

Core Organizers: Strategic authority, initiation of new projects, external representation.


Professional Precedents

Analogous to the journeyman stage in guild systems: entrusted with substantial responsibility, not yet
master but clearly beyond apprentice.

Comparable to the junior officer or tribune in military structures: tasked with overseeing multiple units
while still reporting upward.


Trajectory of Advancement
Successful interns may be elevated to Domain Officers, with the ability to initiate projects, command their
own volunteer teams, and participate in strategic planning. The internship program thus serves as both a
training ground and a filter for future leadership within the Immanent Domain.

Summary
The internship program establishes a professional middle tier in the organization. Interns are not
temporary helpers but emerging leaders: individuals entrusted with responsibility for continuity,
oversight, and integration across projects.
