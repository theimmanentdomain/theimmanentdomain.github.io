               30-Second Script: Why That Matters to Everyone

    FADE IN: A dimly lit stage. A single spotlight on ED PANKOV, standing at a
                                    microphone.

                                        ED PANKOV​
                             (leaning in, voice low, confessional)​
                         Why seek to be the greatest poet of all time?

                                      (pauses, smirks)​
                         My confession? I don’t actually believe I am.

                                      And that matters.

                               Because if I did believe it—​
         if I sat on the throne of my own mind, self-anointed, self-crowned—​
                               what hunger would remain?​
                       What poem would dare to wake me at night?​
                       What line would seduce me into obsession?


                               (leans in closer)​
                    The poet who thinks he’s the greatest—​
                               is already dead.​
                         But the poet who doubts—​
                                  still listens.

                                  So tell me—​
                      What do you believe about yourself?​
                        And is it making you sharper?​
                       Or is it the knife at your throat?

                               FADE TO BLACK.


             Poem in Your Style: "The Lie of the Throne"

                          I do not sit upon the throne,​
                            I do not wear the crown—​
                if I did, my words would crack like old glass,​
            my hands too full of glory to grasp the weight of a pen.

                                      No—​
                        I am the beggar-king of poetry,​
                        roaming the streets of language,​
                     searching for a lost scrap of meaning,​
                    an unspoken prayer I’ve yet to whisper.

                       Let me kneel before the poem—​
                        for the poet who stands too tall​
                         has already stopped listening.

 Musical Interlude: “Tom Waits for No One” (Revised Lyric Flow for
                          Performance)

            (Soft guitar strumming, a low hum before the verse kicks in.)

                     🎶 Tom waits for no one, my friend—​
                   Here we go again, back around the bend.​
                              Confessions are cool,​
                Foolish lives are up to the devil to comprehend.

                     And so and so onto these confessions—​
                                I’ll rip them up,​
                            Forget about all of them,​
                        And go back on the road again.

                                 Tom waits, no.​
                              Time waits for no one,​
                           Friends, enemies—again.     🎶

            The Alluring Invitation to Tonight’s Gathering

                            (Soft, knowing smile.)​
                      And so—where does that leave us?

Somewhere between belief and deception, between confession and performance.​
                 Perhaps tonight, you’ll come to my home.​
                              To see my bird.​
      To discuss enlightenment, self-liberation, truth, love, and magic.

                        Mostly, to live life as a poem.

                     And whether or not I tell the truth—​
              Well, that depends on what you believe about me.

                      And what I believe about myself.

                                   (Pause.)

                      Tom waits for no one, my friends.​
                        So—what are you waiting for?

                             FADE TO BLACK.
